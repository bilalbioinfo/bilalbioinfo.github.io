My main work as a postdoc is **[DNAharvester](https://github.com/NBISweden/DNAharvester)** — a modular, reproducible [Nextflow](https://www.nextflow.io/) pipeline I developed at the Centre for Palaeogenetics for analysing highly degraded DNA from ancient and historical specimens. It tackles the core bioinformatic challenges of ancient DNA — short fragments, low endogenous content, post-mortem damage, and cross-species contamination — through metagenomic pre-filtering, competitive and adaptive mapping, and systematic evaluation of reference bias, together with subworkflows for mitogenome assembly, taxonomic classification, genetic sex determination, and variant calling. It is the project I am most proud of, and one I hope will make rigorous analysis of degraded DNA accessible to the wider community.

I am a computational biologist working at the intersection of **computational biology, palaeogenomics, and population genetics**. I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the [Centre for Palaeogenetics](https://palaeogenetics.com/), Stockholm University, and, since January 2026, Lead Computational Biologist at **GenomeVault**. My research recovers meaningful biological signal from deep-time samples to study the ecology, population dynamics, and evolutionary history of different species — including *Homotherium latidens*, mammoths, and ancient equids — and I am interested in metagenomic approaches to ancient environments and in integrating multi-omics data to reconstruct past ecosystems.

Alongside DNAharvester, I build and maintain open-source tools for iterative mapping, adapter cleaning, capture-probe design, and SNP network reconstruction. On this site you will find my [publications](/publications/), a full [CV](/cv/), the [software and repositories](/repositories/) I maintain, and a [blog](/blog/) where I write about bioinformatics, ancient DNA methods, and reproducible research.
