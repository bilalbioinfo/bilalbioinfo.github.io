I am a computational biologist working at the intersection of **computational biology, palaeogenomics, and population genetics**. Since January 2026 I am Lead Computational Biologist at **GenomeVault**, and I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the [Centre for Palaeogenetics](https://palaeogenetics.com/), Stockholm University.

I develop bioinformatic pipelines to recover and analyse highly degraded ancient DNA from deep-time samples. By addressing the challenges posed by fragmented and damaged DNA, I aim to gain insights into the ecology, population dynamics, and evolutionary history of different species — including *Homotherium latidens*, mammoths, and ancient equids. I am also interested in metagenomic approaches to studying ancient environments and in integrating multi-omics data to reconstruct past ecosystems and evolutionary histories.

Alongside research, I build and maintain open-source tools — including [DNAharvester](https://github.com/NBISweden/DNAharvester), a Nextflow pipeline for analysing highly degraded DNA, plus pipelines for iterative mapping, adapter cleaning, capture-probe design, and SNP network reconstruction.

On this site you will find my [publications](/publications/), a full [CV](/cv/), the [software and repositories](/repositories/) I maintain, and an occasional [blog](/blog/) where I write about bioinformatics, ancient DNA methods, and reproducible research.
